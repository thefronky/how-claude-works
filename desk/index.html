THE WHOLE TRICK

The desk is small, and it forgets silently. Claude never says "I lost that." It just stops knowing. A long chat isn't a smarter chat — the beginning of it has quietly slid onto the floor.

That's what memory is for. Anything you'd have to repeat, put on the shelf. It costs no desk space and it survives every fall.

A fresh chat is a clean desk, not a factory reset. Hit "start a fresh chat" — the clutter goes, the shelf stays. That's why starting over often makes it sharper instead of dumber.

Repeating yourself is a symptom. If you're saying the same rule for the third time, it fell off. Don't say it louder — put it on the shelf.

"I don't know" comes out sounding like an answer. Ask about Tuesday with no cabinet and you get a confident number that is simply invented — ask twice and it changes. Wrong doesn't look wrong. That's the thing to watch for.

Memory is not a filing cabinet. The shelf is a few facts about you. The cabinet is every row of what happened — and it looks up the one row it needs instead of hauling all 340 onto the desk. Putting records on the shelf just buries the rules.
