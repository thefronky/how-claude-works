THE WHOLE TRICK

It forgets silently. Claude never says "I lost that." It just stops knowing. Your rule was the first thing you said, which makes it the first thing to fall off.

Wrong doesn't look wrong. It didn't ignore you and it didn't apologise — it answered confidently with the rule already on the floor.

That's what memory is for. Switch the shelf on: the card still slides off the desk, but the rule survives and the job comes back clean.

Short chat, same fix. Turn the long conversation off and nothing ever falls. Long chats aren't smarter chats — the start of them is on the floor.
