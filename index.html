THE WHOLE TRICK, IN FOUR LINES

Rules shape everything, silently. The store sent the same kind of stuff every run — YOUR switches decided what survived. Turn "never red" off and red floods back. You never re-typed anything: the rules file does the reminding.

Memory saves wasted trips. With the triangle note ON, Claude didn't even ask for triangles. With it OFF, watch the console: it orders them, the store says OUT OF STOCK, and it has to call again. Same store, dumber shopper.

The store is an "API." Claude can't invent shapes — it goes OUT to get real ones (a store, a weather service, your sales numbers) and then arranges what came back the way your rules say.

It writes down what it learns. Every run can add a note. That's the loop that makes it smarter next week than it was today — swap "shapes" for anything real and it's the same machine.
