THE WHOLE TRICK, IN FIVE LINES

CLAUDE.md shapes everything, silently. The store sells what it sells — YOUR file decided what survived. Turn "never red" off and red floods back. You never re-typed a word.

Memory saves wasted trips. With the triangle note ON, Claude doesn't even ask. With it OFF, watch the console: it orders triangles, the store says OUT OF STOCK, and it has to call again. Same store, dumber shopper.

The Board is where the human stays boss. When the store offers the premium gold square, Claude doesn't decide — it posts the question and waits for your tap. Then your answer becomes a memory, and it never has to ask again. (In real setups, several Claudes work at once and share one board.)

The store is an "API." Claude can't invent shapes — it goes OUT to get real things (a store, a weather service, your sales numbers) and arranges what came back the way your file says.

It writes down what it learns. Every run can add a note. That's the loop that makes it smarter next week than today — swap "shapes" for anything real and it's the same machine.
