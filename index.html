THE WHOLE TRICK

You never wrote a rule. You answered a question. Claude asked your favorite color, you tapped one — that became a memory, and the memory became a standing rule. Same as talking to it.

Rules shape everything, silently. The store sells what it sells; your rules decided what survived. Turn "never red" off and red floods back. You never say it twice.

Memory saves wasted trips. Triangle note ON: Claude doesn't even ask. OFF: it orders triangles, hears OUT OF STOCK, calls again. Same store, dumber shopper.

The Board keeps you boss. On the gold square Claude asks instead of deciding, and waits. Your answer becomes a memory — it never asks again. (In real setups, several Claudes share one board.)

The store is an "API." Claude can't invent shapes. It goes out for real things — a store, a weather service, your sales numbers — and arranges them your way.

It writes down what it learns. That's the loop that makes it smarter next week. Swap shapes for anything real; same machine.
